Technochronology

A History of Technology

Max Terry
consumer@email.com

Machines

Locomotive

1827: Baltimore & Ohio Railroad.

Sewing Machine

1830 (primitive): Barthelemy Thimonnier.
1833 (abandoned): Walter Hunt.
1845 (public demo), 1846 (patent): Elias Howe.

Telegraph

1844 (first major public demo): Samuel Morse.

Electric Fan

1882: Schuyler Skaats Wheeler.
1887 (ceiling): Philip Diehl.

Dishwasher

1889 (handcranked): Josephine Cochran.
1900s (electric).

Air Conditioner

1902: Willis Carrier.

Vacuum Cleaner

1907 (portable electric): James Murray Spangler.

Washing Machine

1910s (invented): Alva Fisher.
1910s (sold): Hurley Co.
1930s (spin cycle): Bendix Corp.

Electric Razor

1928: Jacob Shick.

Processes

Rubber Vulcanization

1890s: Charles Goodyear.

Apparel

Jeans

1873 (patent): Jacob Davis (idea) & Levi Strauss (financing).

Zipper

1893: Whitcomb Judson of Chicago.

Bra

1914: Mary Phelps Jacob.

Sneakers

1917: Charles Goodyear. (First mass-marketed by Keds.)

Nylon

1935 (invented): Wallace Crothers, Du Pont. 1939 (brought to market).

Velcro

1941: George de Mestral of Switzerland.

Disposables

Glass Contact Lens

1888: Eugen Fick, Swiss physician.

Toothpaste Tube

1892: Washington W. Sheffield, Connecticut physician.

Safety Razor

1903: King Gillette.

Deodorant

1903: Anonymous, Philadelphia patent-medicine man ("Mum").

Lip Stick

1915: Maurice Levy, U.S. inventor.

Bandages

1921: Earle Dickson, Johnson & Johnson.

Cotton Swab Sticks

1923: Leo Gerstenzang.

Tampon

1931: Dr. Earle Haas, Colorado physician.

Suntan Lotion

1936 ("Ambre Solaire", first effective sunscreen): Eugene Schueller, L'Oreal founder. 1944 ("Coppertone", cocoa butter and jasmine): Benjamin Green, Miami beach pharmacist.

"Kiss-proof" Lip Stick

1950: Hazel Bishop.

Diapers

1948: Mary Donovan, New York City homemaker. [Sold in 1951 and introduced as Pampers by Proctor & Gamble 10 years later.]

Plastic Contact Lens

1962: Otto Wichterie & Drahoslave Lim.

Connectors

Coaxial

1880: Oliver Heaviside.

N.E.M.A.

1904: Harvey Hubbell II.

D-subminiature

1952: Cannon (acquired by I.T.T. Corporation in 1963).

PS/2

1987: I.B.M..

U.S.B.

1996: Compaq, D.E.C., I.B.M., Intel (including Ajay Bhatt), Microsoft, N.E.C., and Nortel.

Typefaces

Futura

1927: Paul Renner.

Georgia

1993 (designed), 1996 (released): Matthew Carter, Microsoft.

Verdana

1996: Matthew Carter, Microsoft.
